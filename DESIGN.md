# Design System: Midnight Pulse & Neon Coral

## Vision and Narrative
This design system shifts away from earthy utility tones toward a darker, more immersive mood built for online music listening. The interface should feel like a night-stage backdrop for album art, motion, and playback states: deep midnight surfaces create focus, neon coral drives action, and signal cyan adds a clean digital spark for live or active moments. The overall result is more premium, current, and emotionally aligned with streaming behavior.

## Color Palette
The palette is built on layered blue-charcoal surfaces, energized by a vivid coral accent and balanced by a sharp cyan signal color.

*   **Primary (#ff6b57):** A neon coral used for primary CTAs, play buttons, active states, and focal actions. It feels energetic and music-led without becoming overly aggressive.
*   **Secondary (#53657e):** A muted steel-indigo used for secondary buttons, chips, filters, and supporting containers. It keeps the interface modern and polished while staying quiet behind content.
*   **Tertiary (#29d4ff):** A bright signal cyan used for focus rings, live indicators, progress moments, and system feedback. It brings a crisp digital contrast to the darker base.
*   **Neutral (#7b889d):** A cool slate neutral used for borders, low-emphasis surfaces, and subdued text. It softens the dark UI without introducing muddy warmth.

## Typography
The typographic system remains clean and contemporary, with enough personality for an entertainment product.

*   **Headlines:** Plus Jakarta Sans keeps a confident, forward-moving tone that works well with large hero titles and editorial section headers.
*   **Body & Labels:** Inter maintains readability across dense lists, metadata, and control-heavy layouts.

## Structure and Shape
The interface should feel fluid and premium rather than rigidly utilitarian.

*   **Roundedness (Level 2):** Medium corner radii help cards, artwork, controls, and floating surfaces feel more tactile and music-product friendly.
*   **Spacing (Level 2):** Balanced spacing preserves scanability for playlists and content grids while leaving enough breathing room for imagery and emphasis states.

## Design Principles
1.  **Stage-First Contrast:** Keep the background dark and layered so cover art, artist imagery, and playback controls stay visually dominant.
2.  **Coral for Action, Cyan for Signal:** Use coral for anything the user should tap or play; reserve cyan for focus, live, syncing, and system states.
3.  **70 / 20 / 10 Balance:** Use roughly 70% midnight surfaces, 20% cool secondary/supporting tones, and 10% luminous accent color to avoid visual fatigue.
